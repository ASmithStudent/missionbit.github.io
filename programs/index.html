Free Coding Classes for Public School Students

Summer 2014

Want to learn how to make games? There's still space to apply to our free 6 week full time mobile game development class from June 2 to July 11!

By the end of the course you'll have a playable mobile game, ready for release in the app store. You'll get to work with and learn from mentors who are professional software engineers, and get feedback from teams at game development studios.

Students should be in High School, entering High School in the Fall, or have graduated from High School no earlier than 2012. Students must also be able to get to class in San Francisco at 9am. Some programming or computer science experience is ideal. All students meeting the other requirements are eligible to apply, even if not a public school student.

Spring 2014

Mission Bit is teaching 4 classes, 3 high school and 1 middle school, with 68 public school students.
Mission Bit's high school 'Intro to Web Programming' class has a female/male gender ratio of 41% - 59%. Mission Bit high school classes are composed of students attending 9 San Francisco public schools.

Intro Computer Programming

In the 'Intro to Computer Programming' class, students will learn HTML5, CSS3 and Javascript and some core programming concepts through project based instruction. Within the semester the class will build websites and interactive applications. At the end of the semester, the class is broken up into teams, each paired with an instructor and spends a few weeks building a final project. Projects in the Fall semester included an HTML5 game, an interactive light show that change the hue of lights based on sound frequency, a browser-based drawing tool and an instant message app.

Summer 2014 Mentor Opportunities


Mission Bit interns at Hack Reactor

Three Mission Bit spring semester students have been selected to participate in a four week paid internship at Hack Reactor. The interns will spend the first two weeks working on a personal project and the second two weeks working on a project assigned by Hack Reactor. Project work will be assisted by Hack Reactor mentors.

June 2 - June 27, 2014
Days of Week
Monday - Friday

9am - 6pm. 1pm - 2pm is reserved for lunch
Internship Location
  •  Hack Reactor, 944 Market, SF, CA

Mission Bit is looking for mentors to support the summer internship in the following ways.

Serve as remote online tech help for the interns to discuss technical questions they have regarding their project work at Hack Reactor. Hack Reactor mentors will be the front line of help and guidance to the summer interns, but Mission Bit is looking to provide a back up remote tech help channel.

Immersive Mobile Game Development

Mission Bit is running a free 6 week full time mobile game development class from June 2 to July 11! Twelve high school students will learn the fundamentals of android game development and, in the second half of the course, work in teams of 3 or 4 students to produce an 'app store ready' mobile game by the end of the class.

In addition to mentors working with the lead instructor to guide the students through the mobile game development process, Mission Bit will invite guest professionals from top SF mobile gaming companies to work with the class on disciplines such as production, design, monetization, gameplay during the development life cycle.

At the end of class, the teams will present their games for feedback to a panel of game industry professionals that include game publishers, game developers and game industry press.

The summer mobile game development class is supported by Tencent America, DeNA, Glu Mobile and Chartboost.

Course Dates
June 2 - July 11, 2014. No classes on Thursday July 3 and Friday July 4

Class Location
Somewhere in mid-market or SoMA

SFUSD Teacher Intro Web Programming

Mission Bit is running a three week SFUSD Teacher Externship Program supported by Mid Pacific Information and Communication Technologies Center (MPICT). The program includes a week of full day classes and two weeks in a job shadow at an SF based tech company. Upon completion of the classes and job shadow, the teachers will submit a plan to MPICT about how they intend to incorporate what they learned into their high school classes. Five SFUSD teachers from Burton, Thurgood Marshall, Lincoln and Lowell are participating in the 2014 Summer program.

The class portion of the teacher externship will teach SFUSD teachers modern HTML, CSS and a little JavaScript by building portfolio pages via GitHub Pages. The class will also focus on how they can bring these tools into their own classrooms to collaborate with their students to teach them the same skills.

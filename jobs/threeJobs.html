Mission Bit Coding Instructor(s)

Mission Bit is calling on those with a passion for code and community empowerment to join our team and dedicate their skills and knowledge towards uplifting SF Bay Area students and closing the tech divide. Our instructors are usually college students that are computer science majors or recent graduates of coding bootcamps. This is a great opportunity to work closely with high school students and share your enthusiasm for code, insightful advice, and desire to help others. As an instructor, you will be at the forefront of designing creative lesson plans, and connecting with students to provide an engaging and welcoming classroom environment.

This is a part-time position at $37.50 per hour.

Why teach with Mission Bit?

We combine social justice and computer science education to empower minority students with knowledge to navigate an industry that has increasing prevalence in our day-to-day lives. We believe that every student should have access to a STEM education, confidence in their abilities, and enough support to guide them into the tech industry with ease.

Primary Responsibilities:

  • Teach a diverse group of students through a project based coding course covering the fundamentals of HTML, CSS and either java or javascript depending on the coding course.
  • Create an inclusive and casual classroom culture based on the Mission Bit values of social justice, community, accountability, smart risks and love.
  • Ensure students are developing sufficient understanding to build their own website or game project.
  • Create a learning process that effectively utilizes teacher’s assistants and volunteers to help achieve the course learning outcomes.
  • Be open to reflect upon and change your teaching practices to achieve desired student learning outcomes.
  • Be an ambassador of Mission Bit on-site and at field trips or events to further support the development and growth of our org.

Min. Qualifications:

  • Be a college student that is majoring or minoring in Computer Science or have graduated from a coding bootcamp.
  • Be able to pass a technical skills assessment test.
  • Understanding of java or javascript (depending on the course) and programming platforms [please see course links above for specific coding/programming requirements].
  • Values align with our mission and is passionate about sharing their CS knowledge.
  • Punctual, organized, and comes prepared to class through the given term.
  • Available for instructor orientation, off-site field trips, end-of-course events.

How to Apply:

  • Send resume and cover letter to hiring@missionbit.com.
  • Title email subject line as “Summer Instructor - Course(s) Preference - Your Name”.
  • All applicants must be willing to take a technical skills assessment test.
